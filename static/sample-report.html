Medium RULE-029 Sustainability Occupied Hospital, 80 beds
AHJ Plan Review Comment
[RULE-029] Sustainability — Occupied Hospital new construction project does not include a whole-building Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) for embodied carbon documentation as required by CALGreen 2025 Section A5.410.4. For healthcare projects of this scale, LCA reporting is required to document global warming potential (GWP) for structural and envelope assemblies. Per CALGreen 2025 §A5.410.4, EN 15978, HCAI Sustainability Guidelines 2026, ISO 14044, the design must be revised to comply.
Step-by-Step Compliance Fix
1. Review CALGreen 2025 §A5.410.4, EN 15978, HCAI Sustainability Guidelines 2026, ISO 14044. 2. 1. Perform a whole-building LCA using an accepted tool (e.g. One Click LCA, Tally, EC3) complying with ISO 14044 and EN 15978. 2. Document embodied carbon (kgCO₂e) for: concrete, steel, masonry, insulation, glazing, and cladding at minimum. 3. Identify low-embodied-carbon substitutions (e.g. supplementary cementitious materials, recycled-content steel) and quantify carbon savings. 4. Submit LCA summary report with construction documents — include bill of materials and GWP results table. 5. Coordinate with structural and envelope engineers to ensure LCA reflects final design specifications. 3. Resubmit revised drawings for HCAI review.
